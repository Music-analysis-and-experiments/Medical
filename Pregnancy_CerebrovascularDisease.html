Overview

This visualization summarizes key points from the review article 'Cerebrovascular disease in pregnancy and postpartum' by Santos and Aguiar de Sousa. It focuses on diagnosis, management, and recent findings for common cerebrovascular complications in pregnancy and the puerperium. Clinical Management Tools

Brain Imaging in Pregnancy & Puerperium

Modality Recommendation Contrast Use Safety Notes
MRI Modality of choice. 1.5T preferred. Use prudently, only when clinically indicated. Gadolinium crosses the placenta. Limit use to situations where benefit is significant. Breastfeeding can continue. No documented teratogenesis. Reduce noise exposure for fetus. Some suggest delaying until after 1st trimester if possible.
CT Scan Do not withhold in a suspected disabling stroke if MRI is not readily available. Iodinated contrast crosses the placenta. Use only if absolutely required. CTA should not be withheld for suspected LVO. Breastfeeding can continue. Minimal fetal irradiation from head/neck scans, below teratogenic threshold. Consider pelvic shielding. Track cumulative radiation.

Acute Ischemic Stroke Management Flowchart

Pregnant patient with suspected Acute Disabling Ischemic Stroke

Immediate Assessment

Involve both obstetrical and stroke specialists. Maternal health is priority.

Emergency Brain Imaging

MRI preferred, but do not delay for CT/CTA if faster.

Consider Reperfusion Therapies

Informed consent from patient/family crucial. Case-by-case decision.

IV Thrombolysis (Alteplase)

"May be considered when anticipated benefits of treating moderate/severe stroke outweigh risks of uterine bleeding." rTPA does not cross placenta. Caution if stroke complicates preeclampsia. Risk in early postpartum not well established.

Mechanical Thrombectomy (EVT)

"Pregnancy should not be a contraindication" for LVO. Benefit expected to outweigh risk. Minimize radiation exposure with abdominal shielding.

Secondary Prevention in Future Pregnancies

  • History of Preeclampsia/Eclampsia

    Low-dose aspirin (12-28 weeks gestation until delivery). Consider calcium supplementation if dietary intake is low.

  • History of Ischemic Stroke

    Low-dose aspirin is reasonable. For high-risk cardioembolism, LMWH or unfractionated heparin should be continued. Warfarin is teratogenic (esp. 1st tri). Avoid statins.

  • History of Hemorrhagic Stroke

    Optimize long-term blood pressure control. Re-discuss treatment of unruptured aneurysms/AVMs before future pregnancy.

  • History of Cerebral Venous Thrombosis (CVT)

    Prophylaxis with LMWH during pregnancy and puerperium is associated with lower rates of recurrent thrombotic events and miscarriage.
